March 1984 – Release of Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind

Hayao Miyazaki’s film becomes a major success and leads directly to the founding of Studio Ghibli.

June 1985 – Founding of Studio Ghibli

Established by Hayao Miyazaki, Isao Takahata, and Toshio Suzuki after the success of Nausicaä.

1986 – Nausicaä airs on "Friday Roadshow"

The film is broadcast on national TV, marking the start of Ghibli's decades-long presence on Japanese television.

1988 – Release of Grave of the Fireflies

Directed by Isao Takahata, this film becomes one of Ghibli’s most emotionally powerful works, deeply tied to postwar themes.

2003 – Launch of First Full-Scale Ghibli 3D Sculpture Exhibition

The inaugural immersive exhibit uses 3D sculptures to bring Ghibli’s films to life, beginning a new era of Ghibli exhibitions.

2023 – How Do You Live? Released Internationally

Later renamed The Boy and the Heron internationally, it marks Studio Ghibli’s expansion into over 80 countries and regions.

2025 – Exhibition Returns to Tokyo After 22 Years

The evolved 3D sculpture exhibit comes back to Tokyo for the first time since 2003.

2025 – 90th Anniversary of Isao Takahata’s Birth

Special exhibits honor his life and contributions, especially through Grave of the Fireflies.

2025 – 80th Anniversary of the End of the Pacific War

Marking the historical context behind Takahata’s most influential works.

2025 – First Public Display of Hideaki Anno’s Cruiser Maya Layout

Previously unreleased artwork from Grave of the Fireflies is exhibited, connecting Ghibli to other animation legends.

2025 – Exhibit of Harmony Cells

Rare “painting-like” animation cells are showcased to demonstrate Ghibli’s unique animation techniques.

2025 – Toshio Suzuki’s 8,800-Book Library Exhibition

An entire section explores how the books Suzuki read throughout his life influenced Ghibli’s storytelling and production.
